## Classic Rock

Guitar-driven anthems and power-chord classics from ’65–’00—riff-heavy hard rock and melodic hooks without the heavier metal edge, featuring icons like Led Zeppelin, Queen, The Rolling Stones, Tom Petty, Foo Fighters, Guns N’ Roses, Aerosmith, AC/DC and more.

*1965 – 2000*

## Progressive Rock

The mellow, easy-listening side of prog—lush synths, warm Rhodes piano, gentle guitar motifs and sweeping orchestral touches that showcase Pink Floyd’s dreamy soundscapes, Genesis’s melodic introspection, Yes’s softer harmonies and King Crimson’s subtler textures.

*1967 – 1980*

## Neo-Progressive Rock

’80s/’90s melodic prog revival with lush synths and emotional storytelling—Marillion, IQ, Arena, and modern torchbearers like Moon Safari.

*1983 – present*

## Classic Pop

Chart-topping ’80s–’90s pop anthems with irresistible hooks and powerhouse vocals—think Michael Jackson, Madonna, Whitney Houston, ABBA, and icons alike.

*1980 – 2000*
## Punk Rock

High‑energy punk and ska‑punk from first‑wave pioneers to modern anthems—Ramones, Sex Pistols, The Clash, Bad Religion, Rancid, Green Day, NOFX, Pennywise, blink‑182 and more, plus ska‑punk staples like Ska‑P and Operation Ivy.

*1976 – present*

## Rock Nacional

Rock Nacional Hall-of-Fame (1980–2010): A curated lineup of Argentina’s quintessential rock and alt-rock bands—Soda Stereo, Los Piojos, Turf, Árbol, Los Auténticos Decadentes, Intoxicados, La Mancha de Rolando, Los Tipitos, Andrés Calamaro, Fito Páez, Los Pericos, Attaque 77 and more.

*1980 – 2010*

## Funk & Soul

Brass-laden, groove-heavy funk and soul classics—James Brown’s fire, Aretha’s grace, Stevie’s groove, and every deep-pocket rhythm in between.

*1965 – 1985*

## Indie Pop & Rock

21st-century indie gems combining catchy hooks and sonic experimentation—Tame Impala, Arctic Monkeys, Alt-J, and today’s most adventurous acts.

*2000 – present*

## Golden Hip-Hop

Lyrical storytelling over sample-rich, boom-bap grooves from hip-hop’s golden era—Nas, 2Pac, The Notorious B.I.G., Dr. Dre, Snoop Dogg, OutKast and the pioneering artists who defined a generation.

*1990 – 2005*

## Classic Reggae

Roots-era reggae with conscious lyrics and laid-back grooves—Bob Marley, Peter Tosh, Burning Spear, and the pioneers who put Jamaica on the map.

*1970 – 1985*

## Reggae Rock

A lively fusion of reggae’s offbeat rhythms with the grit of rock and the urgency of punk. This genre blends laid-back grooves, upbeat tempos, and rebellious energy into feel-good anthems and high-octane jams. From coastal surf towns to festival stages, it’s the sound of warm sunsets, smoky air, and conscious vibes. Featuring bands like Sublime, SOJA, Rebelution, 311, Dirty Heads, Pepper, Iration, The Expendables, and Stick Figure.

*1990 – present*

## Y2K Hits

High-energy, genre-blending Y2K anthems that defined a generation—pop icons like Britney Spears and *NSYNC, pop-punk staples Blink-182 and Linkin Park, and rap-rock trailblazers Eminem alongside the era’s biggest guilty-pleasure hooks.

*1998 – 2003*

## Contemporary Pop

Worldwide chart-toppers from 2010–present: the Billboard smash singles and radio staples everyone knows—from Adele’s powerhouse ballads and Bruno Mars’ funk grooves to Ed Sheeran, Taylor Swift, Billie Eilish, Ariana Grande, Dua Lipa and beyond.

*2010 – present*

## Latin Pop

Vibrant, dance-floor–ready Latin pop juggernauts from the ’90s and ’00s—Shakira’s crossover anthems, Enrique Iglesias’s smoldering hooks, Juanes’s storytelling melodies, Julieta Venegas’s bittersweet earworms, Maná’s rock-infused rhythms and more, blending infectious bilingual choruses and sun-soaked beats.

*1990 – 2010*

## Cult Movie & TV Soundtracks

A cinematic time machine of instantly recognizable anthems and theme songs—from “Eye of the Tiger” and “I Don’t Want to Miss a Thing” to cult-TV earworms like Friends’ theme and 2000s teen-movie jams—designed to teleport you back to those iconic on-screen moments.

*1980 – 2010*

## Eclectic Discovery

Genre-bending treasure hunt across funk, hip-hop, indie, lo-fi, and electronic—perfect for curious ears seeking unexpected gems.

*2010 – present*

## Irish Folk & Trad

Timeless Irish storytelling through traditional and contemporary folk—The Chieftains, The Dubliners, and modern troubadours carrying on the heritage.

*1960 – present*

## Aspen Vibes

Soft-rock and adult-contemporary staples that evoke relaxed alpine mornings and cozy lodge afternoons—warm melodies with a mountain-air feel.

*1980 – 2000*

## Acoustic Singer‑Songwriter

Intimate, lyric‑forward acoustic storytelling—fingerpicked guitars, hushed vocals and spare arrangements spanning 60s folk foundations through today’s indie troubadours. A quieter, more confessional lane than Aspen Vibes, focused on stripped‑back performances and emotional clarity.

*1965 – present*
